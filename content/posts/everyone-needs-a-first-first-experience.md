#### From “I am not-Technical” to a “First FIRST” Moment: Why Austin STEM Center Matters

Dean Kamen famously declared FIRST robotics “the only sport where every kid can turn pro.” It’s a playful truth that hints at a profound shift: when students join FIRST, they don’t just build robots—they build identities. They become innovators, team players, and problem solvers. By graduation, FIRST alumni are overwhelmingly likely to pursue careers in STEM, their confidence buoyed by a simple yet transformative mindset: “I can do this.”

Yet here’s the challenge: most students never even start that journey. They see a poster for FIRST Robotics and subconsciously shrug it off. Why? Because long before they ever considered robotics, they internalized a limiting subconscious belief: *“I’m not technical.”*

Austin STEM Center *exists to break* that internal narrative.

### The Power of a “First FIRST” Experience

Pilots know about something called a “discovery flight.” It’s a moment intentionally crafted to inspire people to become pilots—where a curious individual steps into a small plane for the first time, briefly takes the controls, and suddenly goes from deep butterflies in the stomach “*uh, am I actually about to fly an airplane*???” to a shouting with joy and exhilaration “*Holy ****$%!***** I am actually flying an AIRPLANE!!!”. *These discovery flights cause students and adults to want to become pilots, sometimes as a career, but sometimes just for fun. In either case we would have* drastically fewer* pilots without discovery flight programs.

That’s exactly what ASC is designed to do—but our flight deck is a workshop bench in a makerspace, and our flight controls are wires, servos, and screwdrivers. Our “first FIRST” projects are intentionally short, intensely hands-on experiences built to ensure every participant—whether a hesitant middle-schooler or an uncertain adult—feels the thrill of discovery and competence.

Consider one favorite ASC introductory experience: [turning a servo motor, normally an output device, into an input device](https://img1.wsimg.com/blobby/go/9845ff22-e381-4a7f-bef5-7c50b67f6c23/downloads/Servo%20Take%20Apart%20and%20Signal%20Tap.pdf?ver=1740095240162). For most, it begins with cautious uncertainty. But soon, students are tracing the servo’s three wires—red, black, yellow—and decoding their functions with newfound clarity. They pop open the servo’s casing, peer inside, and discover a tiny gear train coupled to a potentiometer forming a closed-loop control system. They take a breath, then layout out the system in parts on the table ready to hack it into a input device, something it was never designed to be.

This moment is electrifying—students who moments before might have quietly thought “this isn’t for me” suddenly realize, “*I just did that. I can actually do this*.”

### Opening Minds, Shaping Careers

Destin Sandlin’s “Smarter Every Day” video, “[I Tried To Make Something In America,](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3ZTGwcHQfLY&t=2510s)” spotlights a critical need: tool and die makers, machinists, and **engineering creativity**. The future is still about shaping our physical world. FIRST Robotics is the creative intersection of mechanical + electronics + code: Mechatronics

That’s precisely why ASC approach matters deeply. We help students see not just opportunities in STEM careers but also discover the simple, profound joy of making something work. It’s a joyful experience—a revelation of their own capability.

### Changing the Story

Our everyday work at ASC is focused on creating transformative “first FIRST” experiences. This means everything from fully-funded field trips for Title I students to immersive workshops for educators who become ambassadors of technical fluency in their own classrooms. Even adults who never imagined soldering wires find themselves giddy with new skills, often mentoring others along the way.

All of our projects—like the servo as input device—will *always* be open-source and available without a paywall or registration. This is our commitment to spreading innovation everyone we will never meet.

### A Sustainable Vision

Long-term, sustainable funding—an endowment—is essential for ensuring ASC can offer consistent, barrier-free access to these transformative experiences. Stable funding means we don’t compromise access, can quickly iterate to improve our curriculum, and extend our reach.

Imagine the impact if every student who walked through ASC’s doors left knowing, unequivocally, “I *can* be technical. I* can* build. I* can* design.” They* don’t* need to love it, but they are no longer subconsciously self-selecting away from STEM careers. Imagine the ripple effects across our community, industry, and culture.

Dean Kamen dreams of a future where society cheers as loudly for young innovators as it does for athletes. Austin STEM Center is the proving ground for that dream, the first step is a lifelong journey of discovery and competence.
