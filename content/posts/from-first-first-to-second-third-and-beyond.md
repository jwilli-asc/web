Danny Beesly, FabLab and Workforce Development, ASC

Building on the powerful idea of a [“first FIRST” experience](/blogf/everyone-needs-a-first-first-experience)—where a single hands-on moment can ignite a lifelong passion for STEM and innovation—this next chapter is about the second, third, and beyond: empowering people of all ages to confidently tackle real-world problems at ever-growing scales.

Too often, people—whether young students or adults—feel disconnected from the challenges around them, convinced that solving meaningful problems is beyond their reach. Many have been sidelined by traditional educational experiences where learning felt abstract, disconnected from anything tangible or relevant to their lives. Others have made undesired decisions because education was not seen as something for them, missing opportunities for growth and change.

My mission is to change that narrative by creating engaging, hands-on experiences that inspire people to take ownership of the issues they care about and build the confidence to solve them—providing second chances and new perspectives on what education can be.

In this approach, education shifts from a process where teachers pull students along, to one where students become deeply inspired and motivated to pull themselves forward. When learners see real value in what they are doing and feel empowered, they take charge of their own educational journey, transforming from passive recipients to active creators and problem solvers. My goal as an educator is not simply to educate, but to mentor and guide—helping students take their learning into their own hands so that, in short order, they become the experts who teach me and others new things.

To inspire people to address ideas, projects, and challenges far beyond what they’ve imagined possible requires a scaffolding approach. My own inspiration came in my mid-20s when I was recruited to join a team tasked with reimagining how cities could be designed and built from the ground up—in a way that is regenerative and benefits all citizens. The project aimed to build 33 brand-new cities in Indonesia. Although it was never fully realized, this experience dramatically expanded my vision for the impact one can make. I want to instill this same sense of mission and confidence in others—that anyone can think this big.

For over 15 years, I’ve seen firsthand how this approach transforms lives. My FabLabs have helped people start businesses, witnessed teenagers teaching 60-year-olds new fabrication technologies, and influenced countless students—some heading to college for engineering, others becoming educators themselves—to grow this vision and help others find value in themselves. Many have launched careers based on what they learned in these spaces, while others simply discovered joy and confidence in creating and learning.

The journey begins with small, accessible projects that spark curiosity and provide tangible success. These experiences act as scaffolding, building skills and self-belief that empower participants to take on increasingly complex challenges. When people realize they can contribute meaningfully, they shift from passive observers to motivated problem solvers.

This approach embraces all ages and backgrounds, including those who have felt marginalized or disconnected from traditional education. Whether a middle schooler or an adult, the goal is to ignite a mindset that says, “I can do this. I can make a difference.” Inspiration fuels motivation, and motivation drives action.

My involvement with the FabCity initiative—a bold vision to enable cities to produce everything they consume—has been a profound catalyst in this work. Building a local FabCity chapter has shown me how community-driven innovation can reshape what’s possible when people have the right tools, mindset, and support.

At its core, this mission is about empowerment through experience. By providing open, inclusive access to maker spaces, workshops, and collaborative projects, we lower barriers and foster a culture of continuous learning and problem solving. This is about more than technology; it’s about cultivating resilience, creativity, and agency.

Imagine a community where everyone—regardless of age or background—feels confident to identify challenges, design solutions, and contribute to a resilient, thriving future. This vision drives every effort to create hands-on opportunities that inspire, engage, and empower.

The path to lasting change starts with that first small success—a spark that builds confidence and launches a lifelong journey of curiosity and impact. Building on the “first FIRST,” this is the next step: helping more people take the second, third, and beyond steps to unlock their potential as innovators, educators, and problem solvers.
